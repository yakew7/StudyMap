# Get software free or discounted as a student

Most of the tools you need for school and side projects are free for students. The trick is proving you are one — usually with a school email address or a photo of your student ID.

## Start with the GitHub Student Developer Pack

The single best starting point. One application unlocks 90+ tools and credits in one go.

Apply at [education.github.com/pack](https://education.github.com/pack). You'll need a free GitHub account and either a school-issued email address or a clear photo of your student ID. Approval usually takes a few hours to a couple of days.

What you get includes: GitHub Pro, GitHub Copilot Pro, JetBrains All Products Pack, Canva Pro (12 months), Microsoft Azure credits, Namecheap domain + SSL, DigitalOcean credits, and much more.

## JetBrains — free professional IDEs

JetBrains gives students free access to every IDE they make: IntelliJ IDEA Ultimate, PyCharm Professional, WebStorm, CLion, Rider, DataGrip, and the rest.

Apply at [jetbrains.com/community/education](https://www.jetbrains.com/community/education/#students) using your school email. If your institution email isn't recognised, you can upload your student ID instead. The licence is free for as long as you're enrolled, and renews annually — they'll email you a reminder. After graduation you get 40% off a commercial licence for two years.

Note: the student licence is for non-commercial use only. Don't use it for freelance or internship work at a company.

## Notion — free Plus plan

Notion's Education Plus plan (normally ₹800/month) is free for students at accredited colleges and universities. Sign up or log in using your institution email, go to **Settings → Upgrade**, scroll down to *Students & Educators*, and click **Get free Education plan**. Your workspace upgrades instantly if your domain is recognised.

If the option doesn't appear, email [team@makenotion.com](mailto:team@makenotion.com) to get your school's domain added.

Direct link: [notion.com/product/notion-for-education](https://www.notion.com/product/notion-for-education)

## Canva — free Pro features

Canva for Education gives K-12 students (and their teachers) free access to Pro features. Your teacher needs to invite you from their Canva Education account — it's school-managed.

If you're in higher education, check whether your university has a Canva for Campus agreement. If it does, log in through your institution's SSO to unlock Pro. Otherwise, the free Canva tier is still solid for most student design work.

Apply via: [canva.com/education/students](https://www.canva.com/education/students/)

## Microsoft and Google

Many schools provide free Office 365 Education (Word, Excel, PowerPoint, Teams, OneDrive) and Google Workspace for Education. Ask your school's IT department or check if your institution email works at [microsoft.com/en-in/education/products/office](https://www.microsoft.com/en-in/education/products/office).

## General tips

- Use your school email wherever possible — it's the fastest proof of student status.
- Keep a clear, well-lit photo of your student ID for services that need manual review.
- Most student offers expire annually and need renewal; set a calendar reminder.
- If your institution email isn't accepted anywhere, try applying with your ID scan instead — most platforms accept this.
